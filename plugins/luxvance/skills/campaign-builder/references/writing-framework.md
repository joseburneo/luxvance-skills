# Campaign Builder — Writing Framework Reference

The strategic writing framework used by `campaign-builder` when generating Copy Starter Kit ideas for each hypothesis. All output must also respect the Luxvance brand voice rules (see `brand-guidelines` skill).

---

## LAYER 1 — 5 STRATEGIC ANGLES

### 1. Problem Offer
**Use when:** Market is problem-aware, offer is clear and tangible, entry offer exists, high-volume campaign.
**Psychology:** "You have X problem. Here's Y solution."
**Avoid when:** Market is unaware or offer is abstract.

### 2. Poke the Bear
**Use when:** Market sophistication is medium or high, competitive pressure exists, cost of inaction is real.
**Psychology:** "You're exposed to a gap."
**Avoid when:** Segment is unaware and fragile · proof is weak.

### 3. The Gift
**Use when:** Entry offer is strong and fast (<48h), audience is skeptical, inbox is crowded.
**Psychology:** "Here's something useful. No strings."
**Avoid when:** Delivery takes more than 48 hours · personalization is weak.

### 4. Social Proof
**Use when:** Proof assets are strong and Segment-matched, Segment is vendor-comparing, risk reduction is key.
**Psychology:** "Others like you already moved."
**Avoid when:** No Segment-relevant proof exists.

### 5. The Minimal
**Use when:** Persona is C-suite, attention span is low, offer is simple, peer-to-peer context.
**Psychology:** "Short. Direct. Respectful."
**Avoid when:** Mid-level managers need detail to act.

## LAYER 2 — 4 STRUCTURES

### Full Structure (4-Block)
1. Hook
2. Value / Context
3. Proof / Differentiation
4. CTA

Best for: most angles except The Minimal.

### Three Ideas (Bullet-Driven)
1. Hook
2. 3 bullets
3. CTA

Best for: operational personas, clear outcome offers.

### One-Liner (Minimal)
3–4 short sentences total.
Best for: The Minimal angle.

### The Question (Question-Led)
1. Question
2. Short insight
3. CTA

Best for: Poke the Bear, consultative sales, NEPQ-style variation.

---

## LAYER 3 — 3 TONE STANCES

### 1. Assertive / Insight-Driven
Confident. Data-backed. Forward-leaning. Best for C-suite + vendor-comparing segments.

### 2. Conversational / Direct
Collegial. Clear. Practical. Default for most B2B cold outreach.

### 3. Humble / Permission-Based
Low pressure. Curious. Disarming. Best for risk-averse segments or cold introductions to senior leaders.

---

## LAYER 4 — HOOK PSYCHOLOGY (6 Triggers)

1. **Risk** — exposure, compliance vulnerability, reputational damage.
2. **Cost** — escalating spend, missed savings, inefficiency.
3. **Speed** — slow processes, bottlenecks, time-to-value.
4. **Opportunity** — revenue leakage, unclaimed market, upside.
5. **Compliance** — regulatory deadline, audit, certification.
6. **Status / Competitive Movement** — competitor action, peer adoption.

The Hook must match the Use Case's "Why Urgent" field. No urgency driver → no viable hook.

## LAYER 5 — UNIVERSAL ESCALATION LOGIC

For a 3-email sequence:

- **Email 1:** Problem or Insight (opening hook + value proposition)
- **Email 2:** Proof or Clarification (credibility asset + specific metric)
- **Email 3:** Urgency + Easy Exit (cost of delay + exit phrasing)

Rules:
- Never repeat the same CTA three times. Each email needs its own friction tier.
- The Easy Exit on Email 3 is non-negotiable. It lowers unsubscribe rate and preserves domain reputation.

For the 2-email version (the Luxvance standard):
- **Email 1:** Problem or Insight + CTA (medium friction)
- **Email 2:** Urgency + Easy Exit + CTA (higher friction)

---

## LAYER 6 — CTA FRICTION MATRIX

**Low friction** (Email 1, The Gift, The Minimal):
- "Worth a look?"
- "Curious?"
- "Relevant?"
- "Want the one-pager?"

**Medium friction** (Email 1 for most angles):
- "Want me to send the benchmark?"
- "Should I share the case study?"
- "Want a 30-day trial to try it?"

**Higher friction** (Email 2 / 3):
- "15 minutes this week?"
- "Can I send a calendar link?"
- "If I give you a 30-day free trial, would you give me 15 minutes? Just reply yes."

The Minimal angle rarely exceeds low-medium friction.

---

## LAYER 7 — OPENING LINE PATTERNS

**Openers that work:**
- Specific observation tied to public data: "I read about the $2M round you raised in March."
- A real question that matches their reality: "Is the cap table already clean for the new investors?"
- A small challenge / tension: "Most founders only spot cap table gaps in the weeks after a round closes."

**Openers that don't work (avoid these):**
- "I wanted to quickly introduce [Client]..."
- "Hope you're doing well."
- "I came across your company and thought..."
- "I hope this email finds you well."
- Any compliment about their "impressive work" or "innovative approach".

---

## LAYER 8 — SUBJECT LINE RULES

- Max 6 words.
- Feels like a colleague wrote it.
- Use the company name if natural.
- No clickbait. No emojis. No ALL CAPS.
- Simple English.

Subject-line patterns that historically work for Luxvance:
- "After your [funding event] — [specific question]"
- "[First name] — still using [incumbent]?"
- "quick [specific topic] question"
- "[Industry signal] — one thought"

---

## LAYER 9 — MISUSE WARNINGS

Do NOT:
- Use Social Proof without Segment-matched proof.
- Use Poke the Bear without a real, documented differentiator.
- Use The Gift if delivery takes more than 48 hours.
- Use The Minimal for directors or managers.
- Use a high-friction CTA in Email 1.
- Use Liquid or spintax in starter copy — that's the team's job downstream.
- Use em-dashes. Ever. (See `brand-guidelines` for full voice rules.)
